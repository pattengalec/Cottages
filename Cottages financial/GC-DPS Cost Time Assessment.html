Electrical zone range is widest due to 100A vs 200A configuration split ($1,080–$1,820). Plumbing range driven by RPZ model selection and local fab cost for divider plate. Data and ventilation are most price-stable — standard catalog components throughout.

Cost breakdown by zone — low / mid / high
Stacked cost per port face by zone

Electrical zone is 35–36% of total cost across all scenarios. Global standards hardware — the Unistrut rail, Roxtec frames, outer panel, mating gasket, key pin, labels, fasteners — represents 10–13% and is the shared infrastructure cost that does not scale linearly with zone count.

Scale economics — cost per port face by volume
Per-face cost as quantity increases

Volume purchasing begins materializing at 10 units. At 50+ port faces the system approaches $2,400/face on the low track — a 38% reduction from single-unit retail. This is entirely component pricing — no design changes required to achieve the reduction.

The 1-hour target is met comfortably at mid scenario with a practiced crew. High scenario (first deployment, unprepared site, leveling required) extends to ~85 minutes — still within a reasonable first-time window. By the third deployment, most crews achieve sub-45-minute completion.

Factory fab time drops sharply after the first 5–10 units as jigs, fixtures, and workflow are established. First-unit time is a one-time investment. Production-rate fab at 50+ units approaches the low end of this range regardless of labor market.
